Power Apps case study

Desk Booking Tool

A company-wide desk booking Power App built from the ground up in one month to replace a costly ServiceNow booking system. The app used SharePoint as the backend, avoided premium licensing, and included a complex interactive floor-map experience powered by Python-generated coordinate mapping.

Built forIntact Financial

Business impact

$500K annual platform cost avoided Helped replace a ServiceNow booking system that cost about $500,000 per year before additional maintenance labor.
Built in one month Delivered a production-ready booking tool quickly enough to meet the business need without waiting on a long vendor implementation.
No premium licensing Used SharePoint as the backend so the solution stayed covered under regular Microsoft licensing instead of requiring premium Power Platform licenses.
Company-wide adoption The tool is being used across the company and continues to evolve with ongoing updates and enhancements.

What made it complex

The hardest part was the interactive map. Desks needed to appear in the correct place on real floor plans, show availability, support filtering by location, area, equipment, and booking date, and still feel simple for employees. I used Python scripting to calculate and manage coordinate placement so the Power App could translate floor-plan locations into clickable desk-booking targets.

Interactive booking map

Users could choose a location, floor, date, area, and equipment, then book from a visual floor plan. The legend made availability clear by distinguishing blocked, assigned, restricted, booked, selected, and available desks.

Home page

The home page gave employees three clear actions: book a space, view their bookings, or search for a colleague. The goal was a simple first screen that made booking fast for regular users.

My bookings

Employees could review confirmed, cancelled, and completed bookings from a self-service dashboard, reducing support requests and giving users visibility into upcoming reservations.

Skills highlighted

  • Power Apps: designed and built the booking interface, map interaction, search experience, dashboard, and user navigation.
  • SharePoint architecture: used SharePoint lists as the backend to avoid premium licensing while supporting booking records and availability logic.
  • Python scripting: generated and managed desk coordinate data so floor-plan desks could be represented as clickable booking targets.
  • Power Automate: supported booking workflow needs, operational handoffs, and update paths behind the app experience.
  • Cost optimization: replaced an expensive ServiceNow booking path with an internal Power Platform solution estimated to avoid about $500,000 per year plus maintenance labor.
  • Enterprise delivery: delivered the first production version in one month and continued improving it for company-wide usage.

Power Apps case study

AI-Enabled HR Onboarding Platform

A centralized onboarding application with a Copilot Studio agent that helps HR, managers, and new hires understand next steps, answer onboarding questions, and manage requests, task ownership, escalations, employee lookups, and process visibility from one governed Power Platform experience.

Built forBMO

What the app does

The app gives onboarding teams a single place to start new hire requests, view current work, review escalations, search for employees, and see the process flow. The Copilot agent acts like a friendly onboarding assistant: it answers common questions, explains what needs to happen next, and routes users toward the right task or request path.

For new hires A Copilot agent explains onboarding steps and reduces repeated HR support questions.
For HR and operations Clear task queues, escalations, search, and process visibility.
For managers Structured onboarding data captured consistently across teams.
For platform governance Role-aware design, reusable data model, and controlled app behavior.

Home screen

The home page separates everyday actions into simple zones: current activity, new records, and search. Users can quickly find their tasks, escalations, new hire requests, reporting, and process views without needing to understand the underlying data tables.

Data model

The model connects Teams, Users, Employees, Task Frames, and Task Data. In plain English: teams own the onboarding task templates, users and employees provide people context, and task data tracks who needs to do what during onboarding.

Task update screen

The task screen guides the user through onboarding steps with a left-side progress path, a central form, contextual field information, and save/submit actions. This design helps users complete work accurately while giving support teams clear process status.

Skills highlighted

  • Power Apps: designed the user interface, navigation, forms, task screens, and business-user experience.
  • Copilot Studio: designed the onboarding assistant to answer common questions, guide users to the right next step, and support self-service intake.
  • Dataverse: structured the data model for employees, users, teams, task frames, and assigned task records.
  • Power Automate: supported notifications, approvals, and workflow handoffs behind the app experience.
  • Power BI: enabled overview reporting and process visibility for onboarding status.
  • Governance: documented architecture, data model, process flow, security controls, and platform considerations.

Built forLoblaw

What the app does

During COVID-19, many employees were sick, isolating, or around people with COVID and could not work. The business did not have a reliable live count of who was on leave because information from 2,000+ stores was moving through emails and files, making the data late and inconsistent. The app gave stores a controlled place to enter leave records immediately so HR had a live feed for government reporting and employee pay protection.

Built in 3 weeks Delivered quickly during a difficult COVID-19 operations period when accurate data was urgently needed.
2,000+ store data problem Replaced delayed emails and files with immediate store-level entry for employees on leave.
Live leave feed Gave HR a more accurate view of employees on leave, eligible pay protection, and reporting volume.
Government and pay accuracy Supported correct government data submission and helped ensure employees on leave were paid properly.

Fast silent demo

Entry screen

HR and store admins used the entry screen to capture employee information, site number, leave dates, pay frequency, contact status, and return-to-work information. In plain terms, this was the controlled intake form for eligibility review.

View records screen

Copilot Studio Intake Agent

Built forIntact Financial

Impact highlights

Reduced manual review Saved an estimated 2 hours of daily request review. At $110/hour and 260 business days, that is about $57,200 in annual effort avoided.
ServiceNow update cost avoided Replacing a continuously updated ServiceNow form avoided roughly 40 hours per update at $300/hour, 3 times per year: about $36,000 annually.
Estimated annual value The two measurable benefits together represent about $93,200 in annualized effort and update cost avoidance.
Team autonomy The team could update questions, branching logic, and form behavior directly in Power Platform instead of waiting on external ServiceNow configuration work.

Copilot Studio agent layer

The Copilot Studio agent analyzes each request after the guided intake path, identifies the request type, summarizes the business need, estimates delivery effort, recommends next steps, and helps assign the work to the appropriate support or delivery team.

Analyzes request context Reviews the selected path, answers, impacted users, urgency, and business objective to create a cleaner triage summary.
Routes to the right team Supports assignment decisions across automation, app build, deployment, support, and governance request types.
Estimates effort Frames likely effort, dependencies, and complexity so the team can prioritize work before manual review begins.
Recommends next steps Prepares action guidance for intake reviewers, including follow-up questions and the practical next move for the requester.

Guided request path

Users started by choosing what they needed: a new automation request, business application request, solution assistance, or production deployment. This kept intake structured and helped route users into the right set of questions.

Company-standard branded front door

The welcome experience was designed to feel like an internal enterprise product, with clean visual hierarchy, familiar branding, and a clear start point for business users submitting Power Platform service requests.

Review before submit

Before submission, users reviewed their request details in one place. This reduced incomplete requests, gave the delivery team cleaner intake data, and supported better Copilot-generated summaries.
